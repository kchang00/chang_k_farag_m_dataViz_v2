Distribution of Medals by Gender

Is the distribution of genders equal? Stats say that men have won more medals than women in the past overall, as well as in individual categories of medals (gold, silver and bronze). That's really too bad, because both genders are awesome sauce (as well as the genders not included in these graphs). People are awesome and should be equal. Hopefully we will see a more even distribution of medals in the future.

Women have less medals than men in every category. It could be because more men are participating in the olympics. However, that is still less than ideal. More comparisons are necessary to determine if the percentage of medals won by men is still larger than the percentage of medals won by women.

Canada Rivalries

Canada reigns supreme in its national winter sport, ice hockey. In fact, we have nearly half of all the gold medals in ice hockey. Isn't that something? Go us.

Canada is also always compared to their neighbours, the United States of America. They have slightly more medals than us, but they do have a population of 327.16 million (2018) vs Canada's population of 36.95 million (2018). That's a population that's 8.85 times bigger than Canada, but do they have even 8 times the medals we do? Of course not. We're almost equal. Because Canada is awesome.
